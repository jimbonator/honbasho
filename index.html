Natsu 2014

May 01: The new yokozuna, Kakuryu, starts off with a shiroboshi victory.
May 02: Yokozuna Hakuho once again shows Endo his strength with an easy win.
May 03: Goeido wins over the undefeated Hakuho and earns himself a valuable star.
May 04: In a battle between those with one loss each, Hakuho is declared the winner.
May 05: Ikioi marks his 11th win and joins the battle for the championship. He receives the second Fighting Spirit Award of this tournament.
May 06: Kisenosato defends his two loss count, and is right behind Hakuho who has just one loss.
May 07: Harumafuji also keeps his loss count at two, and on the 14th day he will face Kisenosato in a battle to defend their two losses.
May 08: Hakuho defeats Kotoshogiku in a close battle, and defends his only loss. Harumafuji, Kisenosato, and Ikioi follow behind, each with two losses.
May 09: Ikioi, who had two losses so far, is defeated and drops out of the battle for the championship.
May 10: Hakuho wins over Kakuryu and defends his one loss count; if he wins tomorrow he will claim the championship. Depending on the Senshuraku, there may be a championship match.
May 11: Kisenosato is victorious in the battle between those with two losses each, and keeps his chances of winning the championship in the Senshuraku.
May 12: Sadanoumi, who just debuted into the Makuuchi Division, had a great score of 10 wins and 5 losses, and receives the Fighting Spirit Award. He leaves behind a new record of both father and son receiving a Sansho Award at their Makuuchi Debut tournament.
May 13: Goeido wins through the Senshuraku matches with a kachi-koshi, defends his position as a sekiwake, and receives his fourth Outstanding Performance Award.
May 14: Ozeki Kisenosato wins over the new yokozuna Kakuryu. If Hakuho is defeated, there will be a championship match.
May 15: Yokozuna Hakuho defeats Harumafuji, and claims the championship title. It has been two tournaments since he last won a championship, and this is now his 29th championship title.
