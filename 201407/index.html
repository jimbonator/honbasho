Nagoya 2014 (July) sumo highlights

01: Aged 35 years and 1 month, Takekaze defeats a yokozuna for his first kinboshi victory. This breaks the record for the oldest rikishi to claim a kinboshi victory, one that his disciple Yoshikaze had just set on the 4th day.

02: Goeido wins over yokozuna Kakuryu and gains a kachi-koshi.

03: As in the previous tournament, Goeido puts an end to Hakuho's undefeated winning streak and wins recognition. He defeats two yokozunas during this tournament.

04: Kotoshogiku uses his skillful "gaburiyori" technique and is at 10 wins and 1 loss. He is in a tie with Hakuho.

05: Takayasu defeats the popular Endo, and claims his 10th victory to remain in the battle for the championship.

06: In a battle between those whom each have just one loss, Hakuho proves victorious and takes the lead.

07: Takayasu's great physical condition and movements allow him to keep his loss count at two, and continues on in the fight for the championship.

08: Kotoshogiku pushes out Osunaarashi, who has defeated two yokozunas, and wins with an oshidashi to keep his loss count at two.

09: Kakuryu is defeated and now has three losses.

10: Hakuho suffers another loss, and is now in line with Kotoshogiku and Takayasu at two losses.

11: In a battle to defend their 2-loss count, Kotoshogiku comes out victorious.

12: Hakuho defeats Kakuryu, and now there are only two rikishi with a 2-loss count: Hakuho and Kotoshogiku.

13: Endo gains a kachikoshi in the Senshuraku.

14: Goeido, who has kept his sekitori rank for 14 consecutive tournaments, defeats Kotoshogiku for a final score of 12 wins and 3 losses, and is awarded his fifth Outstanding Performance Award. With having won 32 bouts in the last three tournaments, he has been promoted to ozeki rank as of July 30th, after the tournament concluded.

15: Yokozuna Hakuho claims his 30th championship. He says, "I am happy to have reached the same success as the two major yokozunas of the Showa Era had."
